# Interview Talking Points

Use this as a quick prep sheet for recruiter screens, hiring-manager calls, and contract discovery conversations.

## One-Minute Positioning

I build practical AI and automation systems that turn messy workflows into auditable software. My portfolio shows local-first AI agents, RAG-grounded document generation, business operations dashboards, deterministic simulation, and trading analytics infrastructure. The common thread is not a specific framework; it is taking a complicated real process and making it reliable, visible, and easier to run.

## Project Stories

### Empire of Drawdown

Core point:

> I turned complex trading replay infrastructure into a visual product experience with deterministic simulation, seeded scenarios, and debrief views.

What to emphasize:

- TypeScript monorepo architecture.
- Simulator, strategy engine, game domain, API, and UI boundaries.
- 169 passing tests observed during the portfolio audit.
- Demo mode avoids live brokerage risk.

Best interview angle:

> This shows I can make complex technical systems understandable without weakening the engineering underneath.

### Job Application Factory

Core point:

> I built a local-first AI workflow where generated application materials come from approved evidence rather than invented claims.

What to emphasize:

- RAG evidence library.
- Mock Demo Mode for safe public review.
- Critic/self-correction concept.
- Temporary data directory used for public capture.

Best interview angle:

> This is the pattern companies actually need from AI: grounded generation, private data boundaries, and validation before output.

### Trades Resource Command Center

Core point:

> I mapped a real staffing operations workflow into a daily command center for owners and recruiters.

What to emphasize:

- Owner Today View, Assignment Forecast, Open Orders, Recruiter Process Tracker.
- Customer upload/session privacy design.
- Sanitized demo capture with generated data only.
- Strong fit for contract work with small businesses.

Best interview angle:

> This shows I can understand a business process and build software that changes how people work every morning.

### Agentic Idea Validation Engine

Core point:

> I built a local evidence ledger that moves ideas through hypothesis, test plan, run, result, and decision memo.

What to emphasize:

- Local-first SQLite/Python app.
- 51 passing tests observed.
- Helps agentic work avoid drift by requiring evidence and decisions.
- Useful as a PM backbone for larger autonomous or multi-agent workflows.

Best interview angle:

> This shows how I think about AI-assisted development: ideas are cheap, evidence and decisions are the product.

### TA Foundation

Core point:

> TA Foundation is a large trading analytics platform that ingests external exports and market data, then produces reports, optimizer packages, and validation artifacts.

What to emphasize:

- Backtest report generation.
- Strategy discovery and validation.
- Weekly optimizer package.
- Deployment Matrix 252.
- Exit-policy and market-data diagnostics.
- Public examples are sanitized to avoid exposing proprietary trading logic.

Best interview angle:

> This shows I can work across a large, real, messy system with long-running external tools, generated artifacts, and strict verification needs.

## Contract-Work Framing

For small-business clients:

> I can turn the spreadsheets, PDFs, emails, and repeated follow-up steps inside your business into a focused internal tool. The first version does not need to be huge; it needs to make the daily workflow visible and reduce missed work.

Good service offers:

- Internal operations dashboard.
- Customer review portal.
- RAG document assistant.
- Workflow automation.
- Data import and normalization.
- AI-assisted document generation with human approval.

## Recruiter-Screen Framing

For software jobs:

Mention:

- Python, TypeScript, Streamlit, Fastify, React, SQLite, local LLMs, RAG, testing, automation.
