Problem

Strategy agents can look promising in isolated tests while still being unsafe to promote. The system needs a way to compare different trading personalities, preserve replay evidence, and make learning adjustments without letting agents rewrite risk controls on their own.

Solution

The League runs as a local Python dashboard over SQLite with roster management, replay queues, evidence panels, promotion gates, and an audit-backed learning patch workflow. Agents such as ORB Breakout, Trend Continuation, and Mean Reversion are scored from replay outcomes before any operator action.

What It Proves

  • Agent orchestration that compares distinct trading styles through one operating surface.
  • Replay-first evidence with PnL, profit factor, drawdown, win rate, and rule-violation signals.
  • Trust controls through train, validation, and holdout splits instead of status-only approvals.
  • Bounded adaptation where learning patches adjust guarded config values and record applied state.
